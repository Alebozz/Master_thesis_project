Thanks for your participation at this survey, here below are the instructions of how the program works:

In this survey you will be presented 15 random samples out of 88 possible samples. 

For each sample you will see 2/3 feature maps images computed using Fisher's score method over PSDs of single runs 
for that subject plus an additional feature map image that is obtained from the concatenation of the single runs

The feature map that represent the concatenation of the single runs has an interactive grid where each cell is clickable
Every cell of the grid correspond to a given pair Channel-Frequency feature for the discrimination of the Both Hands vs.
Both Feet motor imagery problem.

Your task is to select the cells of the grid that you consider to be the most useful for training a classifier that will
be used for distinguishing the two classes.

The application presents you several buttons that can be used to display additional images that can be useful to guide the decision
about the cells to select.

In particular it will be possible to see the ERD/ERS scalogram of each individual channel of the 16 available by simply selecting the corresponding
cell on the 4x4 grid that has the labels of the channels and then clicking the button below the grid to open the windows that will display the plots

Other than that, you will be able to have a look at the ERD/ERS values over time under the form of topoplots in the mu and beta band

In order to confirm the selection of the cells for the given sample you will need to press the enter key.
Once you confirm your choices, all the opened windows for the images will close, the grid with the selected cells will be cleared and a new sample
will be loaded and displayed in order to be evaluated

At the end, the program will automatically close and generate a .txt file that reports all your choices for each analyzed sample.

For any other kind of information, please refer to the GitHub page where you downloaded this program.

For starting the survey, press the enter key
